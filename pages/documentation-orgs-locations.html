Organizations

An organization is any business entity relevant to the healthcare process. Organizations are part of Cerner Millennium Core.

Cerner Millennium can store information on a variety of organizations.

Why? Organizations are relevant to the health care process. Obtaining a maximum amount of accurate information can positively influence patient care.

Traditional vs. Cerner Model

Traditional Model (Business-centric)

Each organization is classified in business terms and sorted hierarchically. Organizations are in a hierarchy.

Cerner Model (Patient-centric)

Each organization is its own entity. Improves flexibility with regards to organization creation and classification. Organizations are NOT in a hierarchy — all exist as peers.

Traditional Model — Hierarchical
Cerner Model — Flat / Peer

Ambulatory Locations

Locations may need to be built as individual organizations within the Millennium database.

Cerner Recommendation: Build ambulatory locations as individual organizations, as it greatly increases Millennium flexibility when using Ambulatory Solutions.

Facility Organization Type Criteria

A facility is an organization that provides patient care. An organization is a Facility if any ONE of these criteria matches:

  1. Physical locations (rooms or beds) are assigned within the organization and/or patient registration occurs
  2. The organization has an interface between Cerner Millennium and a foreign system
  3. Remote Report Distribution (RRD) is received by the organization and is routed to a specific location within the organization

Client Organization Type Criteria

Client represents specific patient populations (billing, reporting). An organization is a Client if any ONE of these criteria matches:

  1. The organization has a separate population of patients
  2. The organization requests work from another organization (the requesting org is the client)
  3. Cerner Millennium Charge Services is used by the organization
  4. Remote Report Distribution (RRD) is received by the organization

Hierarchies within a Facility

Two important hierarchies can be built within Facility organization types:

1. Location (Patient Care Hierarchy)
Part of Cerner Millennium Core. Common to all Cerner Millennium Solutions.
2. Service Resource Hierarchy (Ancillary Hierarchy)
Relevant for specific ancillary departments implementing specific service resources. NOT part of Cerner Millennium Core. NOT common to all Millennium.

The Patient Care Hierarchy

Each level must have its predecessor established. A bed cannot exist without the room, a location, and a building. A room cannot exist without a location. All levels are required.

Design Considerations — One Organization or Two?

When Might Multiple Organizations Exist?

  • Two hospitals may each be built as a separate organization
  • A secured unit (behavioral health/prison) within one hospital can be a separate organization
  • Ambulatory locations should be built as individual organizations

Considerations When Building Multiple Organizations

  • How are patient and encounter identifiers generated?
  • Will organization level security be needed?
  • Will each hospital integrate different legacy systems, having different interfaces?

The Order Catalog

  • Consists of all orderable items and most billable items
  • Core component of Cerner Millennium
  • Used by multiple Millennium solutions
  • Many design considerations

Orders

An order is an item or service a health care provider determines is necessary to assess or treat a patient.

Assessments (Diagnose Problems)

  • Blood gas
  • Consultation
  • EKG

Treatments (Treat an Ailment)

  • Dressing changes
  • Oxygen therapy
  • Medication

Billable Items

A billable item is any item for which a charge can be captured or scheduled.

  • Includes orders (assessments and treatments)
  • Almost every lab, radiology, and pharmacy order has a charge

Exceptions

  • Bed charges — If using registration management tool, handled elsewhere
  • Add-on charges — e.g. courier fees for transporting specimen to reference lab

Additional Billable Items

Time

  • Occupational therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Respiratory therapy
  • Speech therapy

Supplies

  • IV start kits
  • Kerlix rolls
  • Underpads

Catalog/Activity Type Relationships

  • Cerner Millennium defines relationships between specific catalog and activity types for proper solution functionality
  • Clients can define other relationships

Benefits

  • Enhance privilege-level security
  • Allow building mass numbers of orderables quickly

Activity Subtypes

  • Divisions of Activity Types
  • Can be client-defined
  • Specific solutions require Cerner-defined combinations

Examples

  • Anatomic Pathology → AP Processing, AP Reports
  • Radiology → Ultrasound, MRI

Description and Primary Synonym

Required when designing orders:

Description: Required. Official, legal name. Displays on reports and requisitions.
Primary Synonym: Orderable name, most commonly recognized by clinicians. Displays in all Millennium solutions. Consider future projects when creating.

DCP and Ancillary Synonyms (Optional)

DCP (Direct Care Provider) Synonym: Alternate name only seen by direct care provider. Displays in PowerChart and DCP Tools.
Ancillary Synonym: Alternate name only seen by specific ancillary departments. Radiology Management sees specific synonym. Laboratory Management sees specific synonym.
